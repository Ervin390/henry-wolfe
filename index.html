The New Book

The Mindful Angler

A Companion Guide to Fishing for Stress, Burnout, and a Quieter Mind

A quiet companion for the angler who carries more than gear to the water.

Not a how-to book. Not a self-help book. A book that stands beside you on the bank - between the cast and the wait, between the strike and the release - and helps you notice what was already happening. Nineteen short chapters, eight photographs, thirty days of reflection prompts, and one steady reminder: a rod is not meant to carry everything. Neither are you.

For veterans, working professionals, exhausted parents, and anyone who has driven forty minutes before dawn just to stand in cold water and breathe.

About the Author

Henry Wolfe writes quiet books for people who carry hard things. He has fished cold rivers, slow lakes, and the bridges over both. He believes a good companion does not stand in front of you with instructions - it stands beside you and keeps quiet when quiet is better.

He lives somewhere with water nearby. He answers his email slowly.
